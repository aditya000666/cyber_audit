Define HIPAA


Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act



• Passed in 1996
• Provide ability to transfer and continue health insurance coverage for American worker and their families when they change or lose their jobs
• Define “Protected Health Information (PHI)” as health information that
• Identify as individual
• Is maintained and exchanged electronically or hard copy
• The protection would stay with information as long as information is in hands of covered entity or business associate
• Protection applies to individually identifiable information in any form
• HIPPA reduces health care fraud and abuse
• Manage industry wide standards for health care information on electronic billing and process
• Requires the protection and confidential handling of protected health information

• HIPPA uses 2 primary terms
◇ Required
▪ Means given standard is mandatory and must be complied with
◇ Addressable
▪ Means given standard must be implemented by organisation unless assessment analysis conclude than implementation is not reasonable
